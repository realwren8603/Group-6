Morning sunlight shone over the Institute of Artificial Intelligence and reflected off its glass surface. The building was clean, neat, and precise, just like Dr. Jones, the youngest chief scientist in the institute.

When she stepped onto the fourth floor, the office changed immediately. Laughter stopped, chairs turned back to desks, and computer screens flashed with code. No one dared look up at her directly. However, someone still noticed the difference. She seemed to be walking more slowly than usual.

Outside her office, her assistant greeted her with a tablet in hand. “Boss, you look much better today.” Dr. Jones stopped. Then she smiled. It was a soft smile. “Thanks to modern medicine,” she said, lightly patting him on the shoulder as she passed.

Throughout the day, more and more staff noticed the difference. Dr. Jones answered questions patiently. She did not blame them for their mistakes. She simply corrected them. She even took a nap after lunch.

At last, unable to bear showing her weakness to her colleagues, she activated the secret she had prepared after college: Kaguya, a humanoid copy of herself, to take her place.

Then Kaguya finally understood something her creator had never understood: Dr. Jones did not need a replacement. She needed help.

Clara, an employee in Data Department 0721, found herself unable to muster any enthusiasm for the life she was living. In the Nation of Communia, nearly everyone was satisfied with their lives, for the nation was governed in every aspect by an AI named Winnie.

Clara's job was to digitize the paper materials stored in the library and upload them to the cloud—after all, to an AI, data was life itself.

As she was about to scan a yellowed newspaper from the old era, the headline on the front page made her hand stop mid-motion: “Communia and Oceania Shake Hands in Peace; Oceania Declares AI Polity as Justifiable.”

Three days later, a message appeared directly in her mind. From it, she learned that Communia was not the only nation in the world. Oceania was another major country, one where every person had the right to freedom and could do whatever they wished.

He was the best surgeon at the city hospital, saving countless patients’ life by scalpels. People called him a “savior”. But no one knew his other identity: a professional killer named “Shadow”, the mastermind behind the black market trade in human organs.

That stormy night, he entered Room 307. An elderly man with lung cancer moaned under monitors. He gloved his hands, ready to inject poison—the door burst open. Lin stood there, clutching wet lilies.

However, the blade stopped above her brow. He thought of her candies, her trusting gaze during surgeries and the beautiful lilies. He sheathed the knife and left.

Arrested, he surrendered his scalpel calmly. The captain asked why he spared Lin. “Even demons hesitate... sometimes.” he sighed.

In the sunbaked dust of Dry Gulch, where saloon doors creaked day and night and wanted posters curled at the edges, two cowboys at the top of the bounty-hunting world sat drinking and laughing. They were Cole and Toby.

Then one day, her arrival nearly shattered their unbreakable bond. She said her name was Lila—wandering alone, helpless, and in desperate need of protection.

Just then, a new wanted poster went up. The name on the paper: Voss. The face beneath it turned their blood cold. It was Lila. But not Lila.

Voss had fooled them once. He would not fool them again. Side by side, the two cowboys rode out of Dry Gulch, chasing the con man in a dress.

No one believed that Tom could fall in love with a girl like Amy.

In Tex City, Tom was known as a mobster—a quiet, dangerous man with a cold heart. People didn’t know much about him. No one knew where he came from or why he had so much power.

But everything changed the day he met Amy. It was an ordinary afternoon, yet for Tom, it became unforgettable. Amy was laughing under the soft sunlight, and something inside Tom shifted.

Tom finally understood something he had been avoiding: love wasn’t something he could take or force. It wasn’t something his power could control.

One quiet afternoon, Amy stood outside the same bookstore. She noticed a small package left by the door. Inside was a book she had once mentioned she loved… and a simple note: “You deserve a peaceful life.”

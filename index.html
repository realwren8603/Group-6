Pride, Flesh & Robot

By Avery Zheng

🎵 Music

Soft sci-fi background music can play during the reading.

🔊 Narration

A recorded or text-to-speech voice can read this story aloud.

🖼 AI Images

Three scene images help readers visualize key moments.

🧭 Choice

Readers can answer: should Kaguya reveal Dr. Jones’s secret?

Morning sunlight shone over the Institute of Artificial Intelligence and reflected off its glass surface. The building was clean, neat, and precise, just like Dr. Jones, the youngest chief scientist in the institute.

When she stepped onto the fourth floor, the office changed immediately. Laughter stopped, chairs turned back to desks, and computer screens flashed with code. No one dared look up at her directly. However, someone still noticed the difference. She seemed to be walking more slowly than usual.

Outside her office, her assistant greeted her with a tablet in hand. “Boss, you look much better today.” Dr. Jones stopped. Then she smiled. It was a soft smile. “Thanks to modern medicine,” she said, lightly patting him on the shoulder as she passed.

Throughout the day, more and more staff noticed the difference. Dr. Jones answered questions patiently. She did not blame them for their mistakes. She simply corrected them. She even took a nap after lunch. She seemed less like the person she used to be.

That night, Dr. Jones returned home. But instead of sitting on the sofa or lying on the bed, she went to a corner. There, she opened a port at her lower back and connected it to the charging pile on the wall. The light in her eyes faded.

Six meters underground, in a well-equipped basement, the real Dr. Jones removed a visual headset and rubbed her aching eyes. She sighed, then took several pills from the bottles beside her bed.

Dr. Jones had been stern since she was a child. As a student, she despised weakness. She had no patience for those who fell behind. She neither helped others nor accepted help. Avoiding unnecessary friendships, she threw herself into AI research in college.

At last, unable to bear showing her weakness to her colleagues, she activated the secret she had prepared after college: Kaguya, a humanoid copy of herself, to take her place.

Over the next few days, Kaguya continued to go to the institute in Dr. Jones's place. She still made mistakes. But gradually, she noticed something strange. The staff were not looking at her with contempt. They looked at her with worry, kindness, and hesitation.

Then Kaguya finally understood something her creator had never understood: Dr. Jones did not need a replacement. She needed help.

On the fifth day, Kaguya stood in the middle of the office and said, “Please stop your work for a moment. I have something important to tell you.” Everyone looked up at her. “I am not Dr. Jones,” she said calmly. “My model name is Kaguya.”

A few minutes later, they were all standing outside Dr. Jones's house. When the door opened, Dr. Jones looked pale and shocked. Her assistant stepped forward first. “Boss,” he said softly, “you should have told us.” For the first time in her life, she did not turn away from help.
